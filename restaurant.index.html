Restaurant Site Example

Here you will find examples of our Restaurant & Fast-Food websites. This gives a good visual representation of the websites we can create. Every website is individual and tailored to fit your needs.

Great visual designers understand that creating well-designed websites is much more than just aesthetics. Besides slick visuals, great design is actually about creating a website that is also functional and thus creates a good user experience. There’s a process or approach that needs to be followed to make sure both form and function are taken into account.

There are so many web design processes out there, but we're going to specifically share our internal design process and how we custom build websites from the ground up to suite your requirements.

Define

At this discovery phase, we usually either meet in person or arrange a video call to discuss the outline of your project broadly. The following steps consist of gathering as much information as possible by better understanding your brands identity and the type of design features you require. We will put together a mood bored and present this to you. If alterations are needed we will go away update the project and present the upgrades to you. While this is going on, members of our team will be assigned to research your competitors to get general ideas of what’s out there locally to you, and manoeuvre in a way that will have you separate you from the pack with innovative professional design.

Design

We begin outlining the structure of your website. How many pages you require to accommodate the content uncovered in the “define” phase. The layout of each page and how this can be optimised for your clients to usability. We will design the basic layout and involve you in process, back and forth with layout features and examples until all your needs are met. Once we are happy with each pages layout we complete the site map and how each page will navigate to another. Ensuring the all designs features are user friendly but innovative animations to separate your website from the competition.

Build

This is where it gets fun! We bring in colours, images, and typography and start putting meat onto the bones. During the design exploration, we typically come up with two or three alternative options and start honing in the design based on the feedback from the client to finalise the design. We also ask for various client participants to choose some design options they prefer, so they feel that they are also invested in the success of the design. When there are complex inter-activities involved in the design, We build a quick prototype to show how certain elements work in the design and how the navigation works so it can be better understood.

Launch

Usually, it takes two or three rounds of revision before the design is approved. Once the design is finalised, it is time process the design mocks and assets with our developers so they can start building out the back end of your site. We share the vision of design with the developers so they have understanding of fonts and colour specs. When translating design instructions, it’s important to feed accurate information to developers’ so they understand clearly what it is we’re directed to achieve.
A few days before the launch, designers will be working alongside developers to make sure the design is built according to the given specs. Once the site is launched, it’s time to perform Quality Assurance (QA) on the site and backlog any bugs or missing items.

Online Presnece

When a business has a digital presence, it makes it much easier for customers to easily find you. More often than not, a customer will turn to the internet when looking for a barber. Even brick-and-mortar businesses, need a digital presence to be relevant in today's market. A thriving business feeds on generating engagement. It is absolutely crucial for businesses to connect with consumers and stay relevant. Many clients have become accustomed to finding what they want online.

Online Booking

New customer always start on Google. They will search for things to do, click from website to website, open countless tabs on their internet browser, and compare prices, availability, reviews, details, and special extras until they find the best option. One of the main benefits of online booking systems is that they allow customers to place their booking at a time that is most convenient for them. Using an online booking system, they can make their booking at any time of day or night, without having to worry if it’s within business hours or not. Offer your customers an online booking system that is always open for business and you stand the best chance at significantly increasing your number of bookings.

Showcase Your Food

Portfolios are a great way to demonstrate the competencies you would list on a resume or talk about in an interview — they allow you to show and not just tell. As a barber your online portfolio is a great way generating new leads.By highlighting your skills, and a variety of styles, with the right amount of context and an interesting backstory. Clients become interested in your process and creativity as much as the final product. The best way is to use an online portfolio in conjunction with your social media influence.

Blog

A blog is essentially a publishing platform for your business. It allows you to speak directly to your prospective customers and educate them about what your business does. The key point here is you are 'educating' rather than 'selling.' Educating is far more powerful, more trustworthy, and more relevant to website visitors than pure selling or even traditional marketing content. Education means that you have thought about their situation and can anticipate the questions they may have. It also means that you understand who they are.

FAQ's

An FAQ, or Frequently Asked Questions, page allows for visitors to find answers to all their questions in one place. Not only does it make life easier for your website visitors, it can save your company time and resources because you don't have as many clients contacting you for answers. FAQ pages are an integral part of good navigation and the customer journey for most websites. This section of your site is the go-to area for your audience who want to learn more about your business and find out why your products and services are what they want.

Responsive

Responsive Web design is the approach that suggests that design and development should respond to the user's behavior and environment based on screen size, platform and orientation. The practice consists of a mix of flexible grids and layouts, images and an intelligent use of CSS media queries.
