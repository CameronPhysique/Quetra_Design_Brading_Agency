Drop Caps

A drop cap is short for “dropped capital.” It’s the large capital letter you sometimes see at the beginning of a paragraph in print and digital publications. This decorative element is usually the size of two or more lines of text in a paragraph. We use drop caps to break up sections of an article and signify the starting point of each for the reader. They usually take up the space of two or three lines of text, as seen in most publications.

Pull Quotes

In website design, a pull quote (also known as a lift-out pull quote) is a key phrase, quotation, or excerpt that has been pulled from an article and used as a page layout graphic element, serving to entice readers into the article or to highlight a key topic..

Block Quotes

The Block Quotes are used to display the long quotations. The blockquote element is used to indicate the quotation of a large section of text from another source. It changes the alignment to make it unique from others. Therefore standing out from other text elements on the webpage.
